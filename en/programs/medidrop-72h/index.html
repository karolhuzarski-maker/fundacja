Educational program

MediDrop 72 h

A municipal module for contact, emergency communication and continuity of critical medicines during temporary community isolation, blackout, severe winter weather, flooding or limited road access.

About the program

MediDrop 72 h is a pilot program of the Civil Resilience Foundation designed to support local communities in maintaining contact and access to critical medicines during the first 72 hours of a crisis. The program is intended for municipalities, volunteer fire brigades, local support points, pharmacies, primary care providers and vulnerable residents, including seniors, people living alone and chronically ill patients.

What the program includes

  • Contact 72 — a procedure for checking on vulnerable residents after a crisis event.
  • Signal 72 — a neighbourhood emergency contact chain based on simple messages, OK / HELP / MEDICINES cards and short-range PMR communication.
  • MediDrop Box — a demonstrational kit for medicine continuity exercises, including a container, labels, seals, handover card and temperature monitoring.
  • Municipal exercise — a scenario involving winter isolation, blackout or blocked road access, allowing the local team to practise contact, needs assessment and support coordination.

Goal

The goal of the program is to create a practical support module for the existing municipal crisis management system. The program does not create a new crisis staff and does not replace emergency services, pharmacies or healthcare providers. It provides procedures, training, checklists, signalling kits and an exercise scenario to help identify who needs contact, medicines, assistance or evacuation.

Target groups

  • municipalities and local authorities,
  • volunteer fire brigades and local support points,
  • schools, community centres and village leaders,
  • pharmacies and primary care providers,
  • seniors and chronically ill residents,
  • local leaders and residents of areas exposed to temporary isolation.

Outcome for the municipality

After the pilot, the municipality receives the MediDrop 72 h procedure, a trained local team, a map of communication and risk points, signal cards, Signal 72 kits, a MediDrop Box and an exercise scenario that can be repeated in future years.
